Physical Recovery

Faster muscle recovery

  • Cold constricts blood vessels, slowing the inflammatory cascade that causes post-exercise soreness
  • As you re-warm, fresh oxygenated blood flushes out waste products and accelerates tissue repair
  • Reduces DOMS significantly at 24, 48, and 96 hours post-exercise — backed by a 17-trial meta-analysis
↓ DOMS at 24–96 hours post-exercise
Bleakley et al., BJSM 2012 [1]
Machado et al., 2016 [2]
Mental Health

Mood, stress, and resilience

  • Cold exposure triggers a sharp release of norepinephrine — the neurotransmitter behind mood, focus, and stress control
  • A single immersion can raise norepinephrine levels by 200–300%
  • Consistent practice linked to significant reductions in anxiety and depression over six weeks
+300% norepinephrine increase
Shevchuk, Med. Hypotheses 2008 [3]
van Tulleken et al., PLOS ONE 2023 [4]
Inflammation

Reduced inflammation

  • Regular cold exposure modulates the inflammatory response by lowering pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-6)
  • Anti-inflammatory markers (IL-10) increase in response — helping the body balance its own immune activity
  • Radboud University research showed measurably lower inflammatory markers after an endotoxin challenge in cold-exposed subjects
↓ IL-6 pro-inflammatory cytokine
Kox et al., PNAS 2014 [5]
Ihsan et al., Sports Med. 2016 [6]
Sleep

Deeper, better sleep

  • Core body temperature naturally drops as you enter sleep — cold immersion accelerates this thermal shift
  • Linked to improvements in sleep onset time, total duration, and the quality of deep (slow-wave) sleep
  • Athletes using cold recovery protocols consistently report improved sleep quality the following night
↑ SWS slow-wave deep sleep
Mooventhan & Nivethitha, 2014 [7]
Hausswirth et al., PLOS ONE 2011 [8]
Focus & Cognition

Sharper focus and mental clarity

  • Cold activates the sympathetic nervous system, producing a catecholamine surge that heightens alertness immediately
  • Neuroimaging shows cold exposure activates the prefrontal cortex — the seat of executive function and sustained attention
  • Regular exposure trains the body to modulate this stress response more efficiently over time
PFC prefrontal cortex activation
Huberman, Stanford 2021 [9]
Yankouskaya et al., Biology 2023 [10]
Metabolism

Metabolic activation

  • Cold activates brown adipose tissue (BAT) — a specialised fat that generates heat by burning energy, not storing it
  • Regular exposure increases both the volume and activity of BAT, with downstream benefits for metabolic rate
  • Improvements in insulin sensitivity comparable in some measures to moderate exercise (Cell Metabolism, 2014)
↑ BAT brown adipose tissue activation
van der Lans et al., 2013 [11]
Hanssen et al., Nature Med. 2015 [12]

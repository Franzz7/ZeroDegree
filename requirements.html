Requirements

What you need
at home.

A cold plunge setup requires a few basics. Here's exactly what's needed — and what we check before we book your installation.

01
Outdoor Power Supply

The chiller unit requires a standard outdoor power socket (13A). The socket should be located reasonably close to the installation area — typically within a few metres — and must be a properly installed, outdoor-rated socket.

Extension leads are not suitable as a permanent power solution. If you only have an indoor socket nearby, please let us know when you enquire and we can advise.

02
Outdoor Tap

A standard outdoor tap is needed to fill the tub on installation day and to top up the water level during service visits if required.

The tap should be accessible to our team during service visits. Most standard UK garden taps are suitable.

03
Suitable Outdoor Space

The tub needs to be positioned on a level, stable, hard surface. Suitable options include:

Patio, decking (load-bearing), concrete, paving slabs, or similar firm, flat ground.

Soft ground such as lawn or gravel is not suitable without additional preparation. We'll advise if there are any concerns about your outdoor area when you enquire.

The tub requires a footprint of approximately [[TUB_DIMENSIONS]] — please check this before enquiring. We'll confirm exact dimensions when you get in touch.

04
Safe Property Access

Our team needs to be able to bring the equipment from the delivery vehicle to the installation area. This means:

A gate or access route wide enough to manoeuvre the tub through — typically at least 80cm clear width. A reasonably direct and flat route from the access point to where the tub will be positioned.

If your property has restricted access (e.g. steps, narrow passages, steep gradients), please mention this when you enquire and we'll let you know if it's workable.

Before We Book

We confirm suitability first.

We don't book delivery until we've confirmed that your home is suitable. This protects your time and ours — we don't want to turn up on installation day and find something doesn't work.

When you enquire, we'll ask about your power supply, tap, outdoor space, and access. In some cases we may want to arrange a brief visit to the property before confirming the booking. We'll let you know if that's needed.

If your property doesn't currently meet the requirements, we'll tell you honestly rather than trying to make it work.
